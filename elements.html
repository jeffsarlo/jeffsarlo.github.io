My name is

Jeff Sarlo

An accomplished Financial Operations and Project Management professional for over 20 years. After leaving TD Ameritrade in 2018, I took a short break to contemplate what I wanted to do in this next stage of my career. Blockchain was one technology that I wanted to learn more about, but after reading a book on Blockchain and undertanding less than half of what I read (most of the content was about cryptography) I decided to join Blockgeeks.com to see if their online content offered this information from a different perspective. The Blockgeek courses started out as I had hoped, but I quickly hit a roadblock because 3rd course required knowledge of Javascript. So I went to Barnes & Noble to pick up a book on Javascript only to find out when I got home that HTML & CSS were a necessary prerequisite. Back again to Barnes & Noble to pick up a beginner's guide to HTML & CSS...

...Fast foward a year an a half, six books, a dozen online courses, and countless tutorials later and I can proudly say that I am more than comfortable with HTML, CSS, Javascript and React. I can effectively utilize javascript/css libraries such as jQuery, Bootstrap, Materialize and I have a working knowledge of Node.js, MongoDB and Express.

There are many talented programmers out there today, but what sets me apart is my profesional experience. As someone who has made a career of keeping a finger on the pulse of users of technology, I have developed a keen 'sense' from a laymans perspective. This I will use to my advantage when designing, creating or collaborating in this field.
